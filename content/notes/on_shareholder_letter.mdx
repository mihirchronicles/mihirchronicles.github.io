Behind every great business is a leader who can think clearly, communicate honestly, and build for the long term. Shareholder letters and memos are unfiltered and often remarkably candid.

This is an interactive guide to the most influential shareholder letters and memos ever written. Drawn from the world's finest operators and investors, these documents offer a masterclass in the principles that separate enduring businesses from fleeting ones: disciplined capital allocation, long-term thinking, rigorous risk management, and the unglamorous, compounding work of building something that lasts.

Unlike earnings calls or press releases, the best shareholder letters don't perform, they teach. Warren Buffett explaining why he passed on a deal. Jeff Bezos laying out his philosophy on failure. A founder walking shareholders through a difficult year with unflinching honesty. These are the documents serious students of business return to again and again.

Explore the letters. Study the thinking. Learn from the best.
